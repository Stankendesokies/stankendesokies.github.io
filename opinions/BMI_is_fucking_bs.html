The Body Mass Index (which I will just call "BMI" for the rest of this article) is widely used in the medical world, anyone who has ever gone to a hospital would know. Medical professionals just love to measure your BMI. Oh, and in case you don't know what the hell "BMI" is: it's you weight (in kg) devided by your height (in m) to the power of 2. Which is to say that it's a really simple formula.

Which is just a little strange, cuz like, BMI is supposed to calulate rather or not you're healthy.If your BMI is less than 18.5 you're "underweight", 18.5 to 24.4 is "normal weight", 24.5 to 29.9 is "overweight", and anything above 30 is "obese". So, this very simple, not at all complex formula is used to calculate some pretty big stuff. For example: if you're at an endocrinologist because you've finally confinced your gender "specialist" that you're trans enough to recieve hormones. But your BMI is too low or too high the endocrinologist could still deny you your hormones, because you first need to "get healthier". (This nearly happened to me, and I know people who have had this happen to)

"Ok, that sucks, but it makes sense right? If you're unhealthy some medicine can really fuck you up." Right, that makes sense. Except no it fucking doesn't. The human body is complex as fuck, and a simple formula that takes literally only 2 variables into account could never accurately calculate something as fague as "healthiness". Just think about it for like 30 seconds. Someone who runs marathons and shit all the time would definitely be healthy, but they'd also have a lot of muscle mass. The BMI formula doesn't know the difference between muscle mass and fat mass, so this extremely healthy person might still be considered "overweight" or even "obese" according to BMI.

And I didn't even get into the history of BMI yet. The guy (and yes, ofc it was a man) who invented BMI played a really massive role in early fucking eugenics.1) He (his name was Adolphe Quetelet) did some measurements on some Scotish en French men, and based on those statistics decided that their average weight to height ratio was "ideal" and "normal". Later on Francis Galton used Quetelet's work to create eugenics.2)
